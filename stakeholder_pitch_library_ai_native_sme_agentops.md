## 0. How to use this document

This is a reusable pitch library. It aggregates the stakeholder narratives developed across the Airwallex, SmartCore, Swiss SME holding-company, and agents-first discussions.

Use it in four situations:

1. **Validation conversations** — with FourTrade, SmartCore, Marcus / MIC Invest, wealth managers, treasury teams, or Swiss SME founders.
2. **Pilot conversations** — when asking for access to one workflow, anonymised data, or a controlled 2–4 week agent pilot.
3. **Investor conversations** — after a pilot demonstrates measurable operating lift.
4. **Internal alignment** — with engineering, sales, marketing, leadership, legal, or operating partners.

The strongest sequencing remains:

> **Build useful workflow agents first → prove operating lift → use the proof to validate an acquisition / holding-company thesis.**

Do not lead with a regulated fund or broad private-equity story. Lead with a practical operating proof.

## 2. Positioning rules

### 2.1 What to say

- We are proving an operating model before asking for acquisition capital.
- AI is the operating advantage, not the asset class.
- Humans stay accountable in regulated and trust-sensitive workflows.
- Agents prepare, check, route, explain, and assemble evidence.
- The first metric is an average operating metric: minutes saved per case, response time, rework rate, manual-review time, clients per employee, or EBITDA margin.
- The goal is not hype, but repeatable operating leverage.

### 2.2 What not to say too early

- “We are launching a private equity fund.”
- “We will automate the whole Treuhand firm.”
- “The agent will make tax, legal, investment, or payment decisions.”
- “SmartCore is a low-risk cash-flow asset.”
- “We will build a Swiss Airwallex.”
- “AI will 10x everything.”

### 2.3 How to frame risk

> We are deliberately keeping the first version inside a controlled operating boundary. The agent prepares and explains work; the human approves. We measure the workflow before and after. We avoid holding client funds, executing payments, submitting filings, placing trades, or making final regulated decisions.

## 9. Stakeholder pitch: SmartCore engineering

### 9.1 Objective

Give engineering the “why” and reduce fear of vague AI scope.

### 9.2 Engineering pitch

> We are not building a bank, a PSP, or a full autonomous fraud engine. We are building a low-latency, evidence-bearing decision-support layer on top of existing SmartCore data.
>
> The agent should ingest transaction, customer, merchant, gate, cascade, chargeback, refund, fraud, alert, and score-generator context. It should output a structured review pack: risk reasons, linked entities, similar cases, evidence, suggested next step, confidence, and human approval requirement.
>
> The design must be deterministic where possible, LLM-assisted where useful, and fully auditable. No hidden writes. No autonomous blocking. No rule changes without approval. Every output links back to source records.

### 9.3 Technical acceptance frame

> The first engineering milestone is not “AI magic.” It is a reliable workflow: given a transaction or alert ID, fetch relevant context, normalise it, produce a structured explanation, log the run, and let a human approve, reject, or annotate the recommendation.

## 22. Objection handling library

### 22.1 “Isn’t this just an AI agency?”

> The first proof may look like a services pilot, but the goal is a repeatable operating layer. We are not selling generic AI automation. We are proving specific workflow agents with measurable before/after metrics that can become a value-creation playbook for owned or partnered businesses.

### 22.2 “Why would a founder sell to you?”

> Many founders care about continuity, staff stability, client trust, and legacy. The pitch is not financial arbitrage. It is staged succession plus operational support: preserve the relationship, document the workflow, reduce founder dependency, and make the business more resilient.

### 22.3 “Is AI safe enough for Treuhand or financial workflows?”

> Not for autonomous judgment. That is why the design is human-in-the-loop. The agent prepares, checks, explains, and assembles evidence. A licensed or responsible professional approves final work.

### 22.4 “Can’t incumbents build this?”

> Incumbents can buy software, but many do not have the operating capacity to redesign workflows, measure before/after performance, integrate tools, and manage change. The edge is operator-led implementation and measured control, not access to a model.

### 22.5 “What is defensible?”

> The defensibility is not the model alone. It is workflow data, operating playbooks, integration know-how, trust with Swiss SME founders, human approval design, and repeated evidence that the same layer improves average operating metrics across businesses.

### 22.6 “Why not start with acquisitions immediately?”

> Because the acquisition thesis becomes much stronger after operating proof. Without proof, it is a story. With proof, it becomes a value-creation playbook.

### 22.7 “Why include SmartCore if the thesis is boring cash flow?”

> SmartCore is not the low-risk cash-flow core. It is a strategic technology/upside asset. The clean split is: Treuhand/accounting/field services form the cash-flow base; SmartCore-style payment infrastructure is a strategic adjacency.

### 22.8 “What if staff resist?”

> The agent should remove repetitive admin, not threaten professional identity. We should measure staff acceptance and keep humans in control from the start.
