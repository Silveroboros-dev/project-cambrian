# Strategic Queue Map

Version: 0.1  
Date: 2026-06-30  
Status: pitch and strategy boundary  

## Purpose

This map formalizes future vertical queues without expanding the active product scope.

The current active queue remains Treuhand/accounting validation. Other queues are used for stakeholder positioning, investor conversations, and future option tracking only.

## Queue Rule

A queue becomes active only after the current active queue has produced real validation evidence or failed clearly.

Do not build agents, connectors, demos, or acquisition materials for a new queue just because the queue is strategically attractive.

## Candidate Filter

Every queue should pass this test before activation:

> Can controlled agents reduce expert or admin time by 30-50% while preserving human approval, client trust, audit trail, and data protection?

If the answer is weak, the queue is probably a software feature idea, not an operating or acquisition wedge.

## Queue 1: Trusted Financial Administration

Contents:

- Treuhand;
- accounting;
- tax;
- payroll;
- HR administration;
- corporate services;
- domiciliation and SPV administration.

Strategic role: first wedge.

Why it fits:

- recurring document and deadline workload;
- client trust;
- compliance pressure;
- owner dependency;
- measurable admin time;
- close to the current Treuhand proof.

Initial agent wedges:

- client document intake agent;
- VAT/tax checklist agent;
- bookkeeping exception agent;
- payroll checklist agent;
- employee onboarding/offboarding agent;
- corporate calendar and filing pack agent.

Payroll / HR admin belongs here immediately as an adjacent wedge, not a separate active queue. It is close enough to Treuhand to strengthen the stakeholder story without distracting from Phase 2.

## Queue 2: Wealth / Treasury / Family Office Ops

Contents:

- wealth managers;
- treasury boutiques;
- family-office administration;
- SPV and owner-liquidity reporting.

Strategic role: personal credibility wedge.

Initial agent wedges:

- portfolio review pack agent;
- IC memo agent;
- liquidity report agent;
- KYC/suitability completeness agent;
- cash-position and approval-pack agent.

Boundary:

- no autonomous investment advice;
- no trading;
- no payment execution;
- no suitability decision by agent.

## Queue 3: Insurance & Risk Intermediation

Contents:

- SME insurance brokers;
- benefits brokers;
- claims/admin brokers;
- risk advisory intermediaries.

Strategic role: best non-obvious roll-up candidate.

Why it fits:

- recurring revenue;
- fragmented advisor market;
- renewal deadlines;
- document-heavy workflows;
- client trust;
- compliance evidence needs.

Initial agent wedges:

- renewal preparation agent;
- coverage gap agent;
- claims evidence assistant;
- carrier comparison pack agent;
- SME risk memo agent;
- advice-process evidence log.

Boundary:

- track as future queue until Treuhand has evidence;
- do not pitch as active product work yet.

## Queue 4: Real Asset Administration

Contents:

- property management;
- STWEG administration;
- rental administration;
- building administration;
- permit support and owner reporting.

Strategic role: second-wave acquisition queue.

Why it fits:

- recurring admin;
- tenant, owner, vendor, and document chaos;
- local trust;
- visible before/after metrics.

Initial agent wedges:

- tenant inbox triage agent;
- vendor dispatch agent;
- owner reporting agent;
- lease deadline agent;
- STWEG agenda and minutes agent;
- maintenance evidence pack agent.

Boundary:

- attractive but not first credibility wedge;
- likely needs a local operator or partner.

## Queue 5: Regulated Financial Infrastructure

Contents:

- payments operations;
- crypto on/off-ramp operations;
- PSP and EMI-style infrastructure;
- AML/KYC operations;
- treasury fintech operations.

Strategic role: high-upside optionality, separate from boring Swiss SME acquisition thesis.

Initial agent wedges:

- payment reconciliation agent;
- dispute and chargeback evidence agent;
- merchant-risk agent;
- transaction monitoring agent;
- source-of-funds pack agent;
- compliance event log agent.

Boundary:

- do not mix this into the conservative Swiss SME cash-flow pitch;
- use governance-first language;
- legal/compliance review required before external commitments.

## Research-Only Queues

These are worth tracking but should not enter the active map yet.

| Queue | Contents | Reason to defer |
|---|---|---|
| Trade / Customs / Cross-Border Ops | Customs brokers, freight-forwarding back office, export documents | Strong agent fit, but more specialized acquisition path. |
| Healthcare Admin | Practice admin, pharmacy admin, Spitex admin, medical billing | High impact, but privacy and liability slow validation. |
| Legal / Corporate Ops | Corporate secretarial, contract admin, filings, legal intake | Good workflow layer; acquisition path needs care. |
| Construction Permit / Engineering Compliance Admin | Permits, fire-safety, energy compliance, municipal correspondence | Document-heavy but more project-based and cyclical. |

## Pitch Use

For stakeholders:

- lead with Queue 1 and the live Treuhand validation kit;
- mention payroll as adjacent;
- mention wealth/treasury only where credibility helps;
- mention insurance and property as future queue optionality;
- keep fintech rails separate;
- avoid implying that all queues are being built now.
