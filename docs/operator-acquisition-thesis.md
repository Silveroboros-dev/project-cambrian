# Operator Acquisition Thesis

Version: 0.1  
Date: 2026-06-30  
Status: strategic boundary document  

## Purpose

This document keeps the future acquisition or holding-company thesis separate from the Phase 2 product build.

The current repo should prove an operating edge first. It should not become a private-equity deck, fund-formation project, or broad "AI for every SME" platform.

## Core Positioning

Use this framing:

> Build an AI-native operating playbook for Swiss service SMEs where succession pressure, margin pressure, documentation workload, owner dependency, and regulatory/admin burden are real. First prove measurable workflow improvement. Then consider acquisitions, minority investments, co-investments, or management-services agreements where the operational upside is visible.

Do not lead with:

- a blind-pool fund;
- a holding company before there is operating proof;
- broad claims that AI will transform margins;
- a multi-vertical story that mixes Treuhand, payroll, insurance, property, wealth, treasury, crypto, and payments as if they are one market.

## Strategic Boundary

The first asset to build is not a capital vehicle. It is evidence of operating alpha.

Operating alpha means:

- a repeatable workflow map for one vertical;
- working agents that handle real or realistic documents, tasks, reports, approvals, and audit trails;
- measured before/after results from real operators;
- evidence that owner dependency, rework, handoffs, or reporting burden can be reduced;
- at least one credible path to an owner conversation, pilot, minority investment, succession buyout, or acquisition.

Until that evidence exists, the acquisition thesis is an option, not the product.

## Phase 2 Evidence Target

The next milestone should produce a before/after operating memo, not a platform narrative.

Minimum evidence:

- 10-20 anonymized Treuhand/accounting cases;
- current manual prep time per case;
- current checklist and missing-item baseline;
- reviewer rating for each agent output;
- evidence IDs for material claims;
- time saved or review burden created;
- failure tags for wrong classification, weak evidence, missing context, noisy gap finding, unsafe draft, or checklist mismatch.

Target proof statement:

> This workflow used to take X minutes per case, required Y handoffs, and produced recurring failure modes A/B/C. With the agent-assisted process, reviewable output is prepared in Z minutes, all material claims link to evidence, and client-facing actions remain human-approved.

If the memo cannot be written honestly after Phase 2, do not pitch acquisitions.

## Stakeholder Story

For an investor, advisor, or owner/operator, the credible story is:

> I am not asking you to back a fund today. I am validating an operator-led acquisition thesis. The market is succession-stage Swiss service SMEs. My contribution is AI-native operating transformation: agents, workflow automation, reporting discipline, and governance. The first milestone is 2-3 measured pilots. If those show recurring time savings, lower owner dependency, and better reporting, then we can structure a specific deal-by-deal acquisition or co-investment.

This keeps the ask small and testable.

## Go / No-Go Gates

### Go

Continue toward pilots if Phase 2 shows:

- reviewer trust in checklist and evidence links;
- at least 20% plausible reduction in manual preparation time;
- fewer unresolved handoffs or clearer ownership;
- client-facing outputs remain review-gated;
- the workflow owner would use the system again;
- at least one owner/operator is willing to discuss a pilot or workflow audit.

### No-Go Or Narrow

Stop or narrow if:

- anonymized data cannot be obtained;
- reviewers do not trust the evidence trail;
- outputs create more review burden than they remove;
- firm-specific exceptions dominate the workflow;
- the product story requires real connectors before manual context packets have proven value;
- the pitch depends on unverified market statistics or legal assumptions.

## Compliance Posture

The operating thesis depends on trust. For Swiss service SMEs, the product must sound governance-first, not chatbot-first.

Required posture:

- role-based permissions;
- explicit approvals for client-facing or regulated outputs;
- audit logs and run traces;
- source evidence references;
- context-packet boundaries;
- memory candidates instead of automatic durable memory;
- security checks for prompt injection, sensitive data, and forbidden actions;
- clear separation between advisory agent output and human decisions.

Legal, licensing, data-protection, and investment-vehicle questions require specialist review before external commitments. The repo should not encode legal conclusions.

## External Claims To Verify Before Public Use

The feedback referenced useful market and regulatory claims, but they should not be treated as verified repo facts until sourced for the specific deck or memo.

Verify before quoting externally:

- Swiss SME succession statistics from official or university sources;
- Swiss SME AI adoption survey figures;
- Swiss SME M&A transaction counts;
- FDPIC guidance on AI-supported data processing under the Federal Data Protection Act;
- FINMA commentary on AI, outsourcing, model risk, data quality, cyber risk, third-party dependency, and reputational risk;
- licensing boundaries for portfolio managers, trustees, collective investment structures, and acquisition vehicles.
- Swiss payroll/HR administration burden and dissatisfaction claims;
- insurance-broker fragmentation and roll-up-market claims;
- property-management digital-maturity claims;
- healthcare-admin, customs-digitization, and construction-permit friction claims.

Until verified, use these only as research leads.
